The Story of the Tiranga

The Indian National Flag, known as the Tiranga, was designed by Pingali Venkayya. It was adopted on July 22, 1947, just before India's independence. The flag consists of three horizontal stripes:

  • Saffron – Symbolizes courage and sacrifice.
  • White – Represents truth, peace, and purity.
  • Green – Stands for prosperity and growth.

The Ashoka Chakra in the center is navy blue with 24 spokes, symbolizing eternal progress and righteousness. The Tiranga is a symbol of national pride, unity, and freedom.

History of the Flag

The journey to our present flag went through several designs since the early 1900s. The first national flag was hoisted in 1906 in Calcutta. Later, various versions emerged during the freedom struggle, including one with the spinning wheel representing self-reliance. The final design was officially adopted on July 22, 1947, and has remained unchanged since.

Great Social Reformers of India

Mahatma Gandhi

Leader of India's non-violent independence movement and a pioneer of truth and peace.

B. R. Ambedkar

Architect of the Indian Constitution and a champion for social justice and equality.

Savitribai Phule

India’s first female teacher and a crusader for women's education and empowerment.

Swami Vivekananda

Spiritual leader who inspired youth towards self-reliance and patriotism.
